## Pricing takeaway for JYT

1. **Transparent pricing is a differentiator.** Every direct competitor in wholesale (NuORDER, JOOR) and PLM (Bamboo Rose/Backbone) hides pricing behind sales calls. Shopify is the only transparent one. JYT should publish clear tiers — it builds trust with ateliers who have been burned by enterprise "call for pricing" games.

2. **Atelier-friendly price anchoring.** The enterprise tools start at thousands/month (Shopify Plus = €2,100/mo; Bamboo Rose is unquestionably higher). JYT's target of independent ateliers (5–200 people) cannot afford that. Anchor JYT's pricing well below enterprise PLM/wholesale tools — ideally a self-serve tier under €100/mo and a pro tier that still undercuts JOOR's likely floor.

3. **Feature-gate, not access-gate.** JOOR gates everything behind a sales call. JYT can differentiate by putting a working product behind a credit-card-up-front tier (like Shopify's self-serve). Use public feature comparison tables (like Shopify and JOOR both do) but actually show the price — JOOR shows the table but hides the price, which is the worst of both worlds.

4. **Name the JOOR/NuORDER opacity in copy.** A line like "No sales calls needed to see what you pay — unlike the enterprise wholesale platforms" directly calls out the competitor pricing dark pattern and reinforces JYT's positioning as the honest, atelier-first alternative.
